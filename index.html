Interesting Facts About Camels!

  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.

Interesting Facts About Chameleons!

  • Chameleons can change their colour to camouflage with the surroundings.
  • Chameleons are not deaf but they do not actually have ear openings.
  • Many species of chameleon have horn-like bumps or crests on their heads.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons!

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

Interesting Facts About A Teddy Bear!

  • The incident of Roosevelt sparing the bear led to the popularity and naming of Teddy bears.
  • The story of Goldilocks And The Three Bears was written in 1837 by Robert Southey who was the Poet Laureate at the time.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Interesting Facts About Pandas!

  • A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Interesting Facts About A Flying Cicada!

  • They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
